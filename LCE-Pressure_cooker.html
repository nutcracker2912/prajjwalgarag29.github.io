Life Cycle Engineeing Case Study : Pressure Cooker Lid

I completed this research project under the guidance of Dr. Paulo Peças (Visiting Professor) from Técnico Lisboa as the culminating event for my second semester master's study program

This project examines the environmental and economic implications of manufacturing pressure cooker lids using steel and aluminum. It investigates critical factors such as material properties, production processes, and lifecycle impacts, addressing challenges like cost efficiency and environmental sustainability. By analyzing real-world production data and employing advanced engineering methods, the study proposes optimized designs that balance functionality and safety while targeting significant market share in Europe. This work is pivotal in promoting sustainable manufacturing practices, contributing to the ongoing transition towards environmentally and economically efficient consumer products.
