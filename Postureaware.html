Smart Posture Monitoring and Feedback System

I completed this project under the guidance of Dr. Stephan Odenwald for the study module Instrumentation 32817.

I led a multidisciplinary team of five students to develop an innovative assistive-technology suit designed to help logistics workers prevent lower back pain caused by poor lifting posture. In collaboration with the Professorship of Sports Equipment Technology, we integrated cutting-edge wearable electromechanical components and hardware (e.g., Micro:bit) into our solution. Using Blender for posture simulations, I oversaw prototyping efforts for both upper and lower body suits, ensuring effective sensor placement through rapid, iterative design.

Leveraging Python’s built-in libraries to capture and display sensor data, our inertial suit relied on the Micro:bit’s three-axis MEMS accelerometer to track movement and posture in real time. This approach eliminated the need for frequent component replacements, delivering a cost-effective, comfortable, and ergonomic solution. I also spearheaded a comprehensive risk assessment feature, enabling real-time monitoring of a worker’s risk state throughout the shift.

My project management led to the suit winning the “Best Project Award” and securing additional funding from the Chemnitz Institute of Technology for continued research. Focused on accessibility, we created a lightweight and portable system that we continue to refine and advance, with the ultimate goal of improving worker safety and reducing chronic back pain in manual load-handling environments.
