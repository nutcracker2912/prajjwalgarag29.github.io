Projection-Based Guidance System for Precision Punching of Fiber-Reinforced TPU Sheets

I’m currently working in a team of five at Vaude Gmbh on an exciting project to make the manual die placement process in manufacturing more efficient and precise. The challenge we’re tackling is ensuring that workers can align cutting dies on polyurethane sheets exactly as designed in the pre-optimized CAD patterns. To solve this, we’re using a projector to overlay the cutting patterns directly onto the sheets, giving workers a clear visual guide. A camera works alongside the projector, using computer vision to confirm the die is correctly placed and providing instant feedback to the worker if adjustments are needed. Our early tests show great results, with accuracy improved by over 20%, material waste reduced by around 15%, and setup times shortened by about 25%.

This project is still ongoing, but we’re already seeing its potential to save significant amounts of material and time while making the process easier for workers. By reducing errors and giving real-time feedback, we’re aiming to improve productivity and minimize waste in a way that’s easy to integrate into existing workflows. Beyond the numbers, it’s been a fantastic opportunity to collaborate with a talented team and explore how technologies like projection mapping and computer vision can make a real difference on the factory floor.
