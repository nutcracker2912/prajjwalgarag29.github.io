Sink Roll Fold Mark Reduction Project

One of the principal challenges I addressed upon commissioning the CGL-2 line was reducing sink roll fold marks. Over time, the submerged sink roll surface accumulates harder Fe-Zn-Al intermetallic deposits, which can lead to visible fold marks on the galvanized strip of thickness 0.4mm. By conducting a thorough root-cause analysis and optimizing key process parameters, I was able to mitigate these deposits, thereby improving product quality and operational efficiency.

During the commissioning of our CGL-2 line, we encountered a 25% rejection rate and a 5% drop in OEE due to sink roll fold marks caused by hardened Fe-Zn-Al deposits on the submerged sink roll. To address this, we evaluated numerous parameters—scraper design, scraper pressure, scraper material, and dynamic scraper movement speed, synchronized with line speed to control the scraping pitch.

Initially, we used an SS-316 scraper that lasted only 2–3 days. We then explored alternative high-performance materials such as Stellite 6, Stellite 21, and triboalloys. In parallel, we redesigned and fabricated a cost-effective scraper structure that enhanced scraping effectiveness and longevity.

These combined efforts significantly reduced rejection rates by 20% and improved overall equipment effectiveness by 4.5%, showcasing our commitment to continuous improvement and operational excellence.The last two images are post implementation where the scraper blades lasted approx. 2 weeks and the reason for sink roll changeover was the acccording to SOP.
