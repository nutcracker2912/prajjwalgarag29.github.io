Project Nischel

This Project was done under the guidance of Prof. Dr. Uwe Götze for the module IT Supported Process Flow Chains.

For this Project we had to suppose that we were interns at Nischel Gmbh which manufactured souvenir busts , I worked with a team to evaluate material flows and process chains for producing the company’s signature "Nischel" busts. The project focused on comparing three manufacturing processes—CNC Milling, Waterjet Cutting, and 3D Printing—using the Umberto® software. My responsibilities included performing sensitivity analysis to understand how different input variables, such as raw materials and energy costs, impacted production efficiency and overall costs. I also carried out Material Flow Cost Accounting (MFCA) and Life Cycle Impact Analysis (LCIA) to evaluate the environmental and economic sustainability of each process.

These efforts helped our team identify the optimal production process based on a balance of sustainability, cost-effectiveness, and functionality. This project significantly enhanced my skills in analyzing complex systems, using advanced software tools, and contributing to data-driven decision-making in manufacturing. In conclusion we we able to provide the management with the best manufacturing alternative and all the costs and environmental impacts using EI'99 for all 3 processes.
